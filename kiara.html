My Dearest Lady Kiara,

I know not whether these words shall find favour with thee, yet I felt compelled to write them nonetheless.

I found great delight in our curious little exchanges within the company of the group. Thou didst ever seem to catch me uttering the most absurd of remarks, and somehow didst make even such nonsense worth sharing. Thou art remarkably easy of spirit, gentle in manner, and possessed of a most uncommon grace.

Yet, if truth be spoken, it was thine ever-changing grey-green eyes that most utterly undid me. They are as tranquil depths into which a man might gaze but a moment too long, only to find himself most willingly drowned. And should such be my fate, I confess I should embrace it without the slightest regret.

Remain ever as thou art, my little angel—for the world is made kinder by thy presence.

Yours, with the utmost admiration,
Daksh
